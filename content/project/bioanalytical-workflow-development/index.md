All of my disease-focused research programmes are built upon a foundation of rigorous bioanalytical method development. Generating high-quality, reproducible, and biologically meaningful metabolic data at population scale requires platforms that are carefully designed, thoroughly validated, and continually refined. This research strand spans the complete metabolic phenotyping roadmap — from sample collection and preparation through instrumental analysis, data preprocessing, quality control, metabolite annotation, and bioinformatic modelling — and represents a cross-cutting capability that underpins every project in my research group.

**Mass Spectrometry Platform Development**

First-author publications in Analytical Chemistry (2012, 2019) describe two foundational methodological contributions. The in-vial dual extraction method (2012) enabled comprehensive and highly reproducible metabolic fingerprinting of plasma by LC-MS from a single sample aliquot, and has been widely adopted internationally. The UHPLC-MS/MS tryptophan pathway assay (2019) provided a fully validated quantitative platform for tryptophan, kynurenine, serotonin, and gut health markers in serum and plasma, and is now incorporated into the International Phenome Centre assay portfolio.

Senior-author work in the Journal of Proteome Research (2023) describes a comprehensive stable isotope dilution targeted LC-MS lipidomics workflow covering over 1,000 lipid species across 20 subclasses, optimised for multicohort population phenotyping and validated across multiple independent clinical cohorts. This workflow forms the standard lipidomics protocol at the Australian National Phenome Centre.

**Microsampling**

A growing focus of my methods work is the miniaturisation of metabolic phenotyping workflows to enable remote and community-based sample collection. Dried blood spot and capillary blood microsampling offer significant practical advantages over conventional venepuncture for large-scale and longitudinal studies. Work published in Analytical Chemistry (2024) demonstrated statistical equivalence between self-administered capillary blood microsamples and standard venous collections for NMR-based lipoprotein analysis. A semi-quantitative 4D-lipidomics approach applied to dried blood spots using UHPLC-HRMS with trapped ion mobility spectrometry was published in Talanta (2025), extending high-dimensional lipid coverage to minimally invasive sample types.

**Metabolite Annotation and Structure Elucidation**

Confident metabolite identification remains a critical bottleneck in large-scale metabolomics. First-author work in Analytical Chemistry (2019) described a systematic workflow for the preparative isolation and structural elucidation of urinary metabolites, integrating HPLC fractionation and multi-dimensional NMR spectroscopy to achieve unambiguous structural assignments. Complementary work characterised and mitigated endogenous matrix interferences in LC-MS-based urine profiling (Analytical Chemistry, 2022), improving data quality and annotation confidence in population studies.

**Quality Control**

Analytical quality control is essential for generating reliable data in large multicentre studies. Senior-author work in Analytica Chimica Acta (2025) systematically evaluated the performance of commercial plasma reference materials as surrogates for pooled study samples in targeted lipidomics, providing practical recommendations for QC strategy design applicable to population-scale research.

**Bioinformatics and Data Modelling**

Metabolic phenotyping generates high-dimensional datasets requiring specialised analytical approaches. My research applies multivariate statistical methods including orthogonal projections to latent structures (OPLS), principal component analysis, and machine learning classifiers to metabolomic and lipidomic datasets. I develop and deliver hands-on R-based bioinformatics training, integrating real research datasets into postgraduate and undergraduate teaching to build practical data analysis capability in the next generation of metabolomics researchers.
